Our Purpose

A School Built on
Noble Principles

Sri Vipassi Dhamma School was established under the sacred guidance of Kande Viharaya, Horana, with a singular mission — to nurture young minds in the timeless teachings of Lord Buddha, fostering a generation rooted in compassion, wisdom, and right conduct.

Every Sunday, our students gather not merely to learn scripture, but to embody the Dhamma — walking the Noble Eightfold Path as a lived experience, not just a concept studied from texts.

We believe that a society transformed begins with a child illuminated by wisdom. Our school is that first lamp.

What We Teach

The Four Pillars of Our Curriculum

📖
Dhamma Studies

Deep engagement with the Tipitaka, Jataka stories, and the life of the Gautama Buddha. Students develop a comprehensive understanding of Buddhist philosophy and ethics.

🧘
Meditation & Mindfulness

Age-appropriate meditation practices introduce students to Samatha and Vipassana, cultivating inner stillness, focus, and awareness from a young age.

🎨
Arts & Culture

Traditional Buddhist arts, Pirith chanting, Bodhi Puja rituals, and Sinhala cultural expression are woven throughout the curriculum to preserve our heritage.

🤝
Compassionate Service

Community service projects, dana activities, and welfare initiatives teach students that wisdom without compassion is incomplete — Dana, Sila, Bhavana.

Our Journey

Milestones in Our Story

✦
Founded
Establishment of Sri Vipassi Dhamma School

Under the sacred patronage of Kande Viharaya, Horana, the school opened its doors to the first cohort of students eager to walk the path of Dhamma.

Early Years
Curriculum Formalized Across 12 Grades

A structured grade-based syllabus was introduced, ensuring progressive Dhamma education from early childhood through to young adulthood.

Growth Phase
Welfare & Media Units Launched

The school expanded beyond the classroom with dedicated units for community welfare and digital media, extending our impact into the broader community.

Recognition
Provincial & National Achievements

Students began winning recognition at provincial and national Dhamma school examinations, reflecting the quality of teaching and dedication of both teachers and students.

Today
500+ Students, Digital Transformation

With over 500 enrolled students and a modern digital attendance and records system, we continue growing — honoring tradition while embracing the tools of our time.
